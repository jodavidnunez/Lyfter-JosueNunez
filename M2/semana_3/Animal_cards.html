Blue sea dragon

The Blue Glaucus, also known as the blue dragon, or blue angel. Despite its mythical name, the Blue Dragon is actually a type of sea slug known as a nudibranch. It floats on the water's surface upside down using a gas-filled sac in its stomach for buoyancy. It also stores venom from its prey, being capable of mixing different venoms from anything it eats.

Mantis Shrimp

Despite their small size, their quick punches have a force of more than 1,500 newtons, which can be compared to the force of a .22 caliber bullet. Mantis shrimp have the best eyes in the animal kingdom, being able to see everything humans see plus infrared and ultraviolet wave lengths. Mantis shrimps can recognize other mantis shrimps through visual cues and even their smell.
